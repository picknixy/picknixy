Understanding the "Metabolic Trap"

Why does weight gain happen so easily in our 30s, 40s, and 50s? It's often due to what biologists call the "Metabolic Trap." Over time, due to processed foods, stress, and environmental toxins, our cells become "deaf" to the signals of insulin. This is known as insulin resistance.

When your cells stop responding to insulin, glucose (sugar) stays in your bloodstream. Your body responds by pumping out even more insulin. Since insulin is a "storage hormone," high levels in the blood act as a lock on your fat cells, preventing them from being burned for energy. You could run for miles, but if your insulin levels are high, your body will choose to burn muscle or slow down your brain function before it touches your fat reserves.
